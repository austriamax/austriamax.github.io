Tarea 15. Pregunta 19.3.

If \(X \in L_1(\Omega,\mathscr{F}, \mathbb{P})\) then \(X \in L_1(\Omega,\mathscr{F}, \mathbb{Q}^B)\) and the conditional expectation of \(X\) given \(B\) is defined as \(\mathbb{E}[X|B] := \int_{\Omega}Xd\mathbb{Q}^B = \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)} \int_B X d\mathbb{P} = \frac{\mathbb{E}[1_BX]}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\). Observe that here \(\mathbb{E}[X|B]\) this is just a number, not a random variable.

Demostración por casos:

CASO: (\(X = 1_A \quad A\in \mathscr{F}\))

\[\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}[X|B] &= \int_{\Omega} Xd\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \int 1_A d\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \frac{\mathbb{P}(A \cap B)}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_B 1_A d\mathbb{P}. \end{align*}\]

CASO: (\(X = \sum_{i=1}^N \alpha_i 1_{A_i} \quad \mbox{con} \quad \alpha_i\in\mathbb{R}, \quad A_i \in \mathscr{F}, \quad i = 1, \dots, n.\))

\[\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}[X|B] &= \int_\Omega X d\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \int\sum_{i=1}^N\alpha_i1_{A_i}d \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^N \alpha_i \mathbb{Q}^B(A_i \cap B)\\\\ &= \sum_{i=1}^N \alpha_i \frac{\mathbb{P}(A_i\cap B)}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}[\sum_{i=1}^N \alpha_i \mathbb{P}(A_i \cap B)]\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_B X d\mathbb{P} \end{align*}\]

CASO: (\(X\) es una v.a tal que \(X \geq0\) y existe una sucesión creciente \((X_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}\) con \(X(\omega) = \lim_{n \to \infty} X_n(\omega) \quad \forall \omega \in \Omega\))

\[\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}[X|B] &= \int_\Omega Xd\mathbb{Q}\\\\ &= \int \lim_{n \to \infty} X_n d \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \lim_{n \to \infty} \int X_n d \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \lim_{n \to \infty} [\frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)} \int_B X_n d\mathbb{Q}^B] && (\mbox{Caso anterior})\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_B \lim_{n\to \infty}X_n d\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_B X d\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ \end{align*}\]

CASO: (\(X\) es una v.a tal que \(X = X^+-X^-\))

\[\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}[X|B] &= \int_\Omega Xd \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \int X^+ - X^- d \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \int X^+d \mathbb{Q}^B - \int X^- d \mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_BX^+d\mathbb{P} - \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_BX^-d\mathbb{P} \quad (\mbox{Caso anterior})\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}[\int_BX^+d\mathbb{P}-\int_BX^-d\mathbb{P}]\\\\ &=\frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)} \int_B Xd \mathbb{P} \end{align*}\]

De todos los casos se concluye que \[\mathbb{E}[X|B] = \int_\Omega X d\mathbb{Q} = \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_BXd\mathbb{P}.\]

PD. \(X \in L_1(\Omega, \mathscr{F}, \mathbb{Q}^B)\).

Por hipótesis \[\mathbb{E}(|X|) = \int_\Omega |X|d\mathbb{P} < \infty.\]

Así \[\begin{align*} \mathbb{E}(|X||B) &= \int_\Omega |X|d\mathbb{Q}^B\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_B |X|d\mathbb{P}\\\\ &\leq \frac{1}{\mathbb{P}(B)}\int_\Omega |X|d\mathbb{P}\\\\ &< \infty \end{align*}\]

Por lo tanto \[X \in L_1(\Omega, \mathscr{F}, \mathbb{Q}^B).\]
